The Rijksmuseum


*Located at the Museum square in the Borough of Amsterdam, the capital city of the Netherlands. The first one to make to our list of best museums in the world is the Rijksmuseum. This museum in Amsterdam is the largest museum in the country and has an eminent collection of art and history. It embraces a massive collection of paintings from the Dutch Golden Age, including the work of Vermeer and Rembrandt. Location: Museumstraat 1, 1071 XX Amsterdam, Netherlands Timings: 9:00 a.m to 5:00 p.m


The Uffizi Galleries


*Located in the beautiful city of Florence, the majority of the exhibit is from the Renaissance period with unique artworks and masterpieces. Uffizi Galleries is another must-visit museum on our list of best museums in the world. It accommodates works of art by great Italian artists such as Botticelli, Giotto, Cimabue, Michelangelo, Leonardo da Vinci and Raffaello. The museum’s massive collection is from all the centuries but the majority of it dates back to the 12th and 17th centuries. Location: Piazzale degli Uffizi, 6, 50122 Firenze FI, Italy Timings: 8:15 a.m to 6:30 p.m, closed on Mondays


The Prado


*Located in the capital city of Spain, Madrid, the Prado museum is known for its finest, richest, and most diverse collection of European art in the world. The museum showcases the finest collections of European arts that range from the 12th century to the 19th century. Majas of Goya (La Maja Vestida and La Maja Desnuda) and Las Meninas by Velazquez are the iconic attractions here. It is also one of the three museums that make up the Golden Triangle of Art, the remaining two are Reina Sofía National Art Centre (Museo Nacional Centro de Arte Reina Sofía) and Thyssen-Bornemisza National Museum (Museo Nacional Thyssen-Bornemisza) Location: C. de Ruiz de Alarcón, 23, 28014 Madrid, Spain Timings: 10:00 a.m to 8:00 p.m


The Museum of Modern Art (Moma)


*Located in the core of midtown Manhattan in New York City, the museum of modern art is recognised as one of the most influential modern art museums in the world. Embracing the best collection of modern art including Monet’s Water Lilies, Picasso’s Les Demoiselles d’Avignon, and van Gogh’s Starry Night. The finest exhibit of MoMa is the building itself making it one of the top tourist attractions in Manhattan. The Museum of Modern Art is home to the greatest collections of modern art in the world and being one of the most famous museums, it offers interesting sceneries of the past days. Location: 11 W 53rd St, New York, NY 10019, United States Timings: 10:30 a.m to 5:30 p.m and 10:00 a.m to 7:00 p.m on Saturdays


The Louvre


*A former royal palace, this museum is recognized as one of the world’s largest art museums and a historic monument in Paris. The Louvre museum accommodates 38,000 objects from prehistory to the 21st century in an area of 72,735 square meters. The museum is famous for its glass pyramid which was added in the main courtyard of the Louvre Palace in 1989. Its exhibits are comprehensive and are from Egypt, classical Greece and Rome, medieval Europe and Napoleonic France origin. It is one of the most-visited and the top museums in the world. Location: Rue de Rivoli, 75001 Paris, France Timings: 9:00 a.m to 6:00 p.m, closed on Tuesdays


The British Museum


*Known for its massive collection of eight million works, which is the largest and most eminent in existence. Its diverse collection originates from all the continents, depicting and documenting the tale of human culture from beginning to present. This museum was established in the year 1753, it is looked up as the museum of human history. It vaunts objects such as the Rosetta Stone, the key to the deciphering of hieroglyphs, and the largest collection of mummies outside of Egypt. Location: Great Russell St, London WC1B 3DG, United Kingdom Timings: 10:00 a.m to 5:00 p.m


Vatican Museums


*Located in the Vatican City of Rome famous for its spiral staircase, the Raphael Rooms and the elegantly decorated Sistine Chapel. Vatican Museums were established by Pope Julius II in the 6th century. The museum accommodates 70,000 works, of which 20,000 are on display. The ceiling was painted by Michelangelo between 1508 and 1512 and is the main attraction of the museum. With such astounding architecture, this is one of the most famous museums in the world. Location: 00120 Vatican City Timings: 8:30 a.m to 6:30 p.m (Monday to Thursday), 8:30 a.m to 10:30 p.m (Friday and Saturday), closed on Sundays


The State Hermitage Museum


*Located in St. Petersburg, Russia, this museum was founded in the year 1794 and is dedicated to art and culture. The State Hermitage is one of the largest museums in the world and accommodates more than 3 million exhibits representing Antiquity, Western Europe, Middle East, Russia and many more epochs, countries and regions. The museum includes masterpieces of great artists like Leonardo da Vinci, Rafael, Ticiano, Rembrandt, Rubens, Matisse and Picasso. Location: Palace Square, 2, St Petersburg, Russia, 190000 Timings: 10:30 a.m to 6:00 p.m, closed on Mondays
